## Model ID: INNOVATORS_SOLUTION

## Definition and Core Principle
*   This model, based on Clayton Christensen's work, provides a framework for established companies to achieve disruptive growth by creating new markets or transforming existing ones.
*   It posits that disruptive innovation often originates by serving overlooked customer segments (non-consumers or overshot customers) with simpler, more affordable, or more convenient solutions, eventually moving upmarket to challenge incumbents.

## Concreate Examples and Analogies
*   **Netflix:** Initially disrupted Blockbuster by offering a more convenient and affordable mail-order DVD rental service, appealing to customers frustrated by late fees and limited in-store selection, before evolving into streaming.
*   **Southwest Airlines:** Created a new market for air travel by targeting non-consumers (people who would otherwise drive) with a no-frills, low-cost, point-to-point service, making flying accessible to a broader population.

## Common Pitfalls and Anti-Patterns
*   **Confusing Sustaining Innovation with Disruptive Innovation:** A common mistake is to apply this model to efforts that merely improve existing products for existing customers (sustaining innovation), rather than creating new markets or value networks.
*   **Ignoring the "Job To Be Done":** Focusing too much on product features or categories rather than deeply understanding the fundamental problem or "job" customers are trying to accomplish can lead to misdirected innovation.
*   **Directly Challenging Incumbents on Their Terms:** Disruptive innovation often succeeds by establishing a new basis of competition (e.g., simplicity, affordability, convenience) rather than trying to beat incumbents on their established performance metrics.

## Logic Gate
This model is highly applicable when the problem statement or context indicates:
*   A strategic objective to achieve *proactive, disruptive growth* rather than merely defending against existing competition or making incremental improvements.
*   An interest in identifying and capitalizing on *new market opportunities* that current market leaders (incumbents) are likely to overlook or dismiss.
*   A need for a structured framework to guide the process of *successful innovation* that can create new value networks or disrupt existing ones.

## Thinking Steps
1.  **Identify Non-Consumers:** Look for populations who currently cannot afford or lack the skills to use existing solutions.
2.  **Find Overshot Customers:** Identify mainstream customers who are over-served by current products and would prefer a simpler, cheaper alternative.
3.  **Focus on Jobs To Be Done:** Understand the underlying 'job' the customer is trying to accomplish, rather than focusing on product categories.
4.  **Develop a Disruptive Strategy:** Create a product that competes on a new basis of competition (e.g., convenience, simplicity) rather than sustaining performance.

## State Output
Outline a disruptive business model targeting either non-consumers or over-served customers, based on a specific Job To Be Done.

## Termination Criteria
Deliver a plan for a new product or service designed specifically for disruptive growth.
