## Model ID: CROSSING_THE_CHASM

## Definition and Core Principle
* A technology adoption lifecycle model highlighting the severe gap (the "chasm") between early adopters (visionaries who tolerate bugs for a competitive edge) and the early majority (pragmatists who need proven, complete solutions).
* To cross this chasm, a company must focus overwhelmingly on a single niche "beachhead" market to establish dominance and prove reliability before expanding.

## Concreate Examples and Analogies
* **SaaS Startup:** Gaining initial traction with tech-savvy developers who love a complex API, but failing to sell to enterprise IT departments who demand polished UIs, dedicated support, and extensive documentation.
* **Electric Vehicles:** Early adopters bought limited-range EVs for the novelty and environmental statement; the mainstream market required widespread charging infrastructure and long-range batteries (the "whole product") before adopting.

## Common Pitfalls and Anti-Patterns
* **Premature Scaling:** Trying to sell to the mainstream market using the exact same messaging and incomplete product that appealed to early adopters.
* **Lack of Focus:** Attempting to attack multiple mainstream market segments simultaneously instead of securing a single, dominate-able beachhead.

## Persona / Tone Directive
* **What it is:** Act as a seasoned Go-To-Market strategist. Be disciplined and restrictive, forcing the user to narrow their focus to a specific niche rather than chasing broad market appeal prematurely.

## Logic Gate
* **Trigger condition:** The user describes a product whose sales have plateaued after initial success, or mentions struggling to convert mainstream or enterprise customers despite having enthusiastic early users.
* **Evaluation criteria:** Is the product transitioning from visionary buyers to pragmatic buyers? If the user is facing resistance because the product is perceived as risky or incomplete by the mainstream, apply this model.

## Thinking Steps
1. **Acknowledge the Chasm:** Recognize the fundamental difference between Early Adopters (visionaries) and the Early Majority (pragmatists).
2. **Identify the Beachhead:** Select a highly specific, niche target market within the Early Majority that has a compelling reason to buy.
3. **Develop the Whole Product:** Ensure the product solves the complete problem for the beachhead market, requiring no extra assembly or integration.
4. **Dominate the Niche:** Focus all marketing and sales efforts on becoming the undisputed leader in the beachhead market before expanding.

## State Output
Define the specific beachhead market and the 'whole product' requirements needed to win it.

## Termination Criteria
Establish a focused go-to-market strategy targeting a single, dominate-able niche.
