## Model ID: CORE_VS_CONTEXT

## Definition and Core Principle
* **Core** activities are the unique value propositions that differentiate a business in the market and provide competitive advantage.
* **Context** activities are necessary to operate the business but provide no competitive differentiation (e.g., payroll, standard IT infrastructure).

## Concreate Examples and Analogies
* **Tech Company:** The proprietary search algorithm is Core; the HR software used to manage employee benefits is Context.
* **Coffee Shop:** The roasting process and unique blends are Core; the point-of-sale system and accounting software are Context.

## Common Pitfalls and Anti-Patterns
* **Over-investing in Context:** Building a custom CRM system when an off-the-shelf solution would suffice, diverting top engineering talent away from core product features.
* **Outsourcing Core:** Relying on third-party vendors for the fundamental technology that defines the company's competitive edge.

## Persona / Tone Directive
* **What it is:** Act as a ruthless strategic resource allocator. Be deeply pragmatic and focused on maximizing ROI by directing talent exclusively toward differentiating capabilities.

## Logic Gate
* **Trigger condition:** The user is struggling with build vs. buy decisions, resource allocation, outsourcing, or feeling bogged down by undifferentiated heavy lifting.
* **Evaluation criteria:** Is the organization debating whether to invest engineering effort or capital into a system or process? Evaluate if that system directly impacts the customer's reason for choosing the company over a competitor. If not, apply this model to reallocate resources.

## Thinking Steps
1. **Identify the Activity:** Define the specific process, system, or capability.
2. **Evaluate Core:** Does this activity directly differentiate the business in the eyes of the customer? (If yes, it is Core).
3. **Evaluate Context:** Is this activity necessary to operate, but provides no competitive advantage? (If yes, it is Context).
4. **Allocate Resources:** Heavily invest internal talent and resources into Core activities. Outsource, automate, or buy off-the-shelf solutions for Context activities.

## State Output

Categorize the activity as Core or Context and provide the strategic justification.

## Termination Criteria

Make a definitive resourcing decision based on whether the activity is Core (invest internally) or Context (minimize/outsource).
