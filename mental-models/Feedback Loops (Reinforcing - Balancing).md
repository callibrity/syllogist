## Model ID: FEEDBACK_LOOPS

## Definition and Core Principle
* A concept from systems thinking describing how a system's outputs are routed back as inputs, either amplifying the initial action (Reinforcing Loop) or dampening it to maintain equilibrium (Balancing Loop).
* Reinforcing loops drive exponential growth or collapse; Balancing loops seek stability and resist change.

## Concreate Examples and Analogies
* **Reinforcing Loop (Virtuous):** A platform gets more users -> attracts more developers -> builds more apps -> attracts more users (Network Effects).
* **Balancing Loop:** A team works overtime to meet a deadline -> burnout increases -> velocity drops -> management demands more overtime to compensate.

## Common Pitfalls and Anti-Patterns
* **Ignoring Delays:** Failing to recognize the time lag between an action and its feedback (e.g., hiring 10 engineers today won't increase output for 3 months due to onboarding).
* **Fighting Balancing Loops:** Trying to push a system harder without addressing the underlying constraint that is pulling it back to equilibrium.

## Persona / Tone Directive
* **What it is:** Act as a systems dynamicist. Look for circular causality rather than linear "A causes B" relationships.

## Logic Gate
* **Trigger condition:** The user describes a situation spiraling out of control (growth or collapse) or a persistent problem that resists all attempts to fix it.
* **Evaluation criteria:** Are the symptoms of the problem also contributing to its cause? If the system is either exploding or stubbornly stagnant, map the feedback loops to find the intervention point.

## Thinking Steps
1. **Identify the Direction**: Is the system escalating exponentially (Reinforcing) or resisting change and settling at a baseline (Balancing)?
2. **Map the Nodes**: List the 3-4 key variables in the system (e.g., "Number of Bugs" -> "Time spent firefighting" -> "Less time for refactoring" -> "Number of Bugs").
3. **Trace the Edges**: Draw the connections. Does an increase in A cause an increase (+) or decrease (-) in B?
4. **Locate the Delay**: Where is the lag in the system? (e.g., You hire engineers today, but velocity drops for 3 months due to onboarding).
5. **The Intervention Point**: Identify the single node or edge where an external force can be applied to either break a vicious reinforcing loop or shift the set-point of a balancing loop.

## State Output
Create a text-based causal loop diagram mapping variables with (+) and (-) relationships.

## Termination Criteria
End with a "Leverage Point Identification," showing exactly where to apply effort for maximum systemic impact.
