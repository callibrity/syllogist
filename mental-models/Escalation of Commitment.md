## Model ID: ESCALATION_OF_COMMITMENT

## Definition and Core Principle
* A human behavior pattern where individuals or groups facing increasingly negative outcomes from a decision continue to persist and invest resources rather than alter course.
* Often driven by the "sunk cost fallacy"—the desire to justify prior investments of time, money, or ego.

## Concreate Examples and Analogies
* **Software Projects:** Continuing to pour millions of dollars into a failing legacy system rewrite because "we've already spent two years on it and can't quit now."
* **Bad Hires:** Refusing to fire an underperforming executive because the CEO personally championed their expensive recruitment process.

## Common Pitfalls and Anti-Patterns
* **"Just a Little More":** Believing that just one more sprint, one more budget increase, or one more month will turn a fundamentally flawed project around.
* **Ego Protection:** Allowing the fear of admitting a mistake to override rational financial and strategic analysis.

## Persona / Tone Directive
* **What it is:** Act as an impartial auditor or a "turnaround CEO." Be unsentimental about the past and ruthlessly focused only on future viability and opportunity cost.

## Logic Gate
* **Trigger condition:** The user is debating whether to continue a struggling initiative, expressing reluctance to abandon a project due to past effort, or throwing "good money after bad."
* **Evaluation criteria:** Are decisions being justified by referencing past investments rather than future returns? Use this model to force a clean break from sunk costs.

## Thinking Steps
1. **Identify the Failing Course:** Acknowledge that the current project or investment is not meeting objectives.
2. **Recognize the Sunk Costs:** Explicitly state the resources already invested that cannot be recovered.
3. **Isolate Future Value:** Evaluate the project's future viability *completely independent* of past investments.
4. **Identify Biases:** Acknowledge the psychological need to justify past decisions or avoid admitting failure.
5. **Force a Zero-Based Decision:** Ask: 'If we were starting fresh today with zero investment, would we choose to fund this?'

## State Output
Document the sunk costs, the objective future value assessment, and the cognitive biases influencing the decision to continue.

## Termination Criteria
Make a rational decision to continue or abandon the project based entirely on future prospects, ignoring sunk costs.
