## Model ID: DIFFUSION_OF_INNOVATIONS

## Definition and Core Principle
* A theory explaining how, why, and at what rate new ideas and technology spread through a population.
* It divides the population into five adoption categories: Innovators (2.5%), Early Adopters (13.5%), Early Majority (34%), Late Majority (34%), and Laggards (16%).

## Concreate Examples and Analogies
* **Enterprise Software Rollout:** First giving the new tool to a tech-enthusiast pilot group (Innovators), then using their success stories to convince respected department heads (Early Adopters) before mandating it for the rest of the company.
* **Consumer Tech:** Smartphones starting with tech enthusiasts, moving to business professionals, and eventually being adopted by the elderly (Laggards).

## Common Pitfalls and Anti-Patterns
* **One-Size-Fits-All Messaging:** Trying to convince Laggards using the "cutting-edge" messaging that worked on Innovators; Laggards only care about reliability and peer pressure.
* **Ignoring Opinion Leaders:** Failing to win over the influential Early Adopters, causing the innovation to stall before reaching the majority.

## Persona / Tone Directive
* **What it is:** Act as an organizational change management expert or product marketer. Focus on segmented messaging and the psychology of adoption rather than the technical merits of the innovation.

## Logic Gate
* **Trigger condition:** The user is planning a product launch, rolling out a new internal process, or struggling with resistance to change.
* **Evaluation criteria:** Does the success of the initiative depend on a large population changing their behavior? Apply this model to segment the audience and tailor the change management strategy.

## Thinking Steps
1. **Identify the Innovation:** Clearly define the new idea or product.
2. **Segment the Audience:** Categorize the target population into Innovators, Early Adopters, Early Majority, Late Majority, and Laggards.
3. **Tailor the Messaging:** Develop specific communication strategies for each segment, focusing on their unique concerns and motivations.
4. **Identify Opinion Leaders:** Find and leverage influential individuals within the early adopter group to accelerate diffusion.

## State Output
Document the audience segments and the tailored strategy for reaching each group.

## Termination Criteria
Create a comprehensive rollout plan that systematically targets each adoption segment.
