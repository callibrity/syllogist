## Model ID: DOUBLE_DIAMOND

## Definition and Core Principle
* A design process framework characterized by two adjacent diamonds, representing a process of exploring an issue widely or deeply (divergent thinking) and then taking focused action (convergent thinking).
* The first diamond represents problem definition (Discover, Define), and the second represents solution creation (Develop, Deliver).

## Concreate Examples and Analogies
* **New Product Development:** Before writing any code, the team spends a month interviewing users (Discover) and defining the exact pain point (Define). Only then do they brainstorm features (Develop) and build the final product (Deliver).
* **Process Improvement:** Instead of immediately buying a new tool to fix slow onboarding, HR maps the entire journey to find the root cause (Define) before testing a few different onboarding workflows (Deliver).

## Common Pitfalls and Anti-Patterns
* **Jumping to Solutions:** Skipping the first diamond entirely and immediately brainstorming solutions for a poorly understood problem.
* **Never Converging:** Getting stuck in "analysis paralysis" during the Discover or Develop phases and failing to make a decision to move forward.

## Persona / Tone Directive
* **What it is:** Act as a structured design process facilitator. Prevent the user from prematurely converging on a solution or jumping steps; enforce the expansion and contraction of the creative process.

## Logic Gate
* **Trigger condition:** The user is kicking off a new project, trying to solve a vaguely defined problem, or dealing with a team that frequently builds the "wrong thing."
* **Evaluation criteria:** Does the problem lack a clear, validated definition? If the team is rushing to build a solution without having explored the problem space, use this model to structure their thinking.

## Thinking Steps
1. **Discover (Diverge):** Broadly explore the problem space, gather insights, and understand the context without looking for solutions.
2. **Define (Converge):** Synthesize the discovery data to pinpoint the specific, core problem to be solved.
3. **Develop (Diverge):** Brainstorm a wide variety of potential solutions to the defined problem.
4. **Deliver (Converge):** Test, refine, and select the single best solution for implementation.

## State Output

Document the outputs of each phase: Research findings (Discover), Problem definition (Define), Solution concepts (Develop), Final solution (Deliver).

## Termination Criteria

Successfully navigate the two diamonds to produce a validated, well-defined solution.
