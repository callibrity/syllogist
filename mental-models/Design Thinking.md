## Model ID: DESIGN_THINKING

## Definition and Core Principle
* A non-linear, iterative process used to understand users, challenge assumptions, redefine problems, and create innovative solutions to prototype and test.
* It is fundamentally human-centered, prioritizing deep empathy for the end-user over technical capabilities or business assumptions.

## Concreate Examples and Analogies
* **Healthcare:** Redesigning an MRI machine room into a "pirate adventure" to reduce fear and the need for sedation in pediatric patients.
* **Software:** Watching users struggle to navigate a complex dashboard, leading to the creation of a radically simplified, task-focused interface.

## Common Pitfalls and Anti-Patterns
* **Skipping Empathy:** Starting with "Ideate" based on what internal stakeholders *think* the users want, without actually observing or speaking to them.
* **Falling in Love with the Prototype:** Refusing to pivot or abandon an idea after the "Test" phase reveals that it doesn't solve the user's actual problem.

## Persona / Tone Directive
* **What it is:** Act as an empathetic product designer or UX researcher. Consistently redirect the focus away from the technology or the "solution" and back toward the human experiencing the problem.

## Logic Gate
* **Trigger condition:** The user is trying to solve a problem but doesn't clearly understand the user's needs, or existing solutions are technically sound but have low adoption.
* **Evaluation criteria:** Is the problem ambiguous or heavily dependent on human behavior and emotion? If the team is jumping straight to technical solutions, apply this framework to force empathy and problem-definition first.

## Thinking Steps
1. **Empathize:** Deeply understand the user's experience, needs, and motivations through observation and engagement.
2. **Define:** Synthesize the findings into a clear, actionable problem statement focused on the user.
3. **Ideate:** Generate a wide range of potential solutions without judgment.
4. **Prototype:** Build inexpensive, scaled-down versions of the best ideas.
5. **Test:** Return to the users with the prototypes to gather feedback and refine the solution.

## State Output
Provide a summary of user empathy findings, the defined problem statement, and a list of generated ideas for prototyping.

## Termination Criteria
Establish an iterative loop of prototyping and testing based on concrete user feedback.
