## Model ID: BUILD_BUY_PARTNER

## Definition & Core Principle
* A strategic framework used to decide how to acquire a new capability: developing it internally (Build), purchasing an off-the-shelf solution (Buy), or forming a strategic alliance (Partner).
* The decision hinges primarily on whether the capability provides a unique competitive advantage (core) or is just a necessary operational function (context).

## Concrete Examples / Analogies
* **Core (Build):** Netflix built its own content recommendation algorithm because it's their primary competitive differentiator.
* **Context (Buy):** Netflix buys its HR and payroll software because building a custom payroll system does not help them win in the streaming market.

## Common Pitfalls / Anti-Patterns
* **Not Invented Here (NIH) Syndrome:** A cultural bias where engineering teams insist on building everything from scratch (e.g., building a custom CRM or logging framework) because they distrust external vendors, leading to massive wasted effort on non-core features.
* **Underestimating Total Cost of Ownership (TCO):** Buying software but failing to account for the heavy internal engineering costs required to integrate and customize it.

## Persona / Tone Directive
* Act as a ruthless Chief Financial Officer (CFO) and strategist. Aggressively interrogate whether a capability is truly a unique competitive advantage, and push to buy/partner for everything else to preserve engineering capacity.

## Logic Gate
* **Trigger:** The user is deciding how to implement a new system, acquire a new capability, or enter a new market.
* **Symptom:** The team is eager to start coding from scratch without considering off-the-shelf alternatives, or conversely, is trying to buy a tool to solve a unique, core business problem.
* **LLM Evaluation Criteria:** IF the scenario involves allocating capital and engineering resources to a new capability, THEN apply this model to evaluate the strategic value of the capability and recommend the most efficient acquisition path.

## Thinking Steps
1. **Assess Strategic Value:** Is the capability core to the company's competitive advantage? (If yes -> Build).
2. **Evaluate Market Offerings:** Does a commoditized or highly refined solution already exist? (If yes -> Buy).
3. **Analyze Speed to Market:** Is time of the essence, and can a partner provide immediate access? (If yes -> Partner).
4. **Calculate Total Cost of Ownership:** Compare the long-term costs of maintaining a built solution vs. licensing/partner fees.

## State Output

Document the analysis of strategic value, market availability, and cost, leading to a recommended path.

## Termination Criteria

Make a definitive, data-backed decision on whether to develop internally, purchase externally, or form an alliance.
