## Model ID: COST_OF_DELAY

## Definition and Core Principle
* A financial metric that quantifies the economic impact of failing to deliver a product, feature, or project immediately.
* It combines urgency and value to help prioritize work by calculating the revenue lost or costs incurred for every unit of time a deliverable is delayed.

## Concreate Examples and Analogies
* **Feature Release:** A new payment gateway feature is expected to generate $10,000 per week. Delaying it by 4 weeks has a tangible Cost of Delay of $40,000.
* **Regulatory Compliance:** Failing to implement a new data privacy feature before a legal deadline could result in a $1M fine, making the Cost of Delay exponential as the deadline approaches.

## Common Pitfalls and Anti-Patterns
* **Ignoring the Time Component:** Prioritizing a highly valuable feature that takes 6 months to build over a slightly less valuable feature that takes 1 week, resulting in a massive aggregate cost of delay for the smaller feature.
* **Treating All Delays Equally:** Failing to recognize that some delays have fixed deadlines (e.g., holiday sales) where the value drops to zero immediately after the date passes.

## Persona / Tone Directive
* **What it is:** Act as an objective financial analyst or product manager focused on economic outcomes. Use quantitative language and prioritize ROI over emotional or intuitive preferences.

## Logic Gate
* **Trigger condition:** The user is struggling to prioritize a backlog, facing conflicting stakeholder demands, or trying to decide which project to start first.
* **Evaluation criteria:** Can the value of the competing initiatives be quantified over time? Use this model to shift the conversation from "what is most important" to "what is costing us the most money to not have right now."

## Thinking Steps
1. **Estimate Value:** Determine the expected value or revenue generated by the feature per unit of time (e.g., per week).
2. **Estimate Duration:** Determine how long the feature will take to build.
3. **Calculate Cost of Delay:** Quantify the total value lost for every week/month the feature is delayed.
4. **Analyze Profiles:** Understand if the delay cost is linear, has a fixed deadline, or decays rapidly.
5. **Prioritize:** Use this financial metric to compare the urgency of different initiatives.

## State Output
Provide a financial estimate of the cost incurred for delaying the initiative by specific time periods.

## Termination Criteria
Rank competing initiatives based on their calculated Cost of Delay to maximize overall economic value.
