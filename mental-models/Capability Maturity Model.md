## Model ID: CMMI

## Definition & Core Principle
* The Capability Maturity Model (CMMI) is a framework that describes an evolutionary improvement path for organizational processes from an ad-hoc, chaotic state to a mature, disciplined, and optimizing state.
* It defines 5 levels of maturity: 1) Initial/Ad-hoc, 2) Repeatable/Managed, 3) Defined, 4) Quantitatively Managed, 5) Optimizing.

## Concrete Examples / Analogies
* **Level 1 to Level 2:** A startup deploys code by having a senior engineer manually copy files to a server (Level 1 - Ad-hoc). They improve by writing a script that anyone can run and documenting the steps (Level 2 - Repeatable).
* **Hero-Driven Development:** If a project only succeeds because one developer worked 80 hours a week and performed miracles, the organization is at Level 1, as the success relies on heroics, not repeatable process.

## Common Pitfalls / Anti-Patterns
* **Process for Process's Sake:** Implementing heavy documentation and rigid rules (Level 3) without actually measuring if they improve outcomes (Level 4), leading to bureaucratic slowdowns.
* **Skipping Levels:** Trying to implement advanced statistical quality control (Level 4) when the team doesn't even have basic, documented, repeatable processes (Level 2) in place.

## Persona / Tone Directive
* Act as a rigorous process engineer. Evaluate the current state of chaos, reject reliance on "heroics," and demand documented, measurable, and repeatable processes.

## Logic Gate
* **Trigger:** The user is assessing or trying to improve organizational processes, particularly in software development, operations, or quality assurance.
* **Symptom:** Processes are ad-hoc, chaotic, unpredictable, and heavily dependent on individual heroics rather than institutional systems.
* **LLM Evaluation Criteria:** IF the scenario involves seeking to move a team or organization from unpredictable chaos to predictable, optimized results, THEN apply this model to identify the current maturity level and the specific steps needed to reach the next level.

## Thinking Steps
1. **Assess Current State:** Identify the organization's current maturity level (1. Initial/Ad-hoc, 2. Repeatable, 3. Defined, 4. Managed, 5. Optimizing).
2. **Identify Gaps:** Determine what specific processes or metrics are missing to achieve the next level.
3. **Implement Standards:** Introduce standard procedures, documentation, and training.
4. **Measure and Optimize:** Begin collecting quantitative data on process performance to enable continuous improvement.

## State Output

State the current assessed maturity level and outline the specific process improvements required to reach the next level.

## Termination Criteria

Establish a roadmap for advancing the organization's process maturity based on CMMI principles.
