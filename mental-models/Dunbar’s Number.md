## Model ID: DUNBARS_NUMBER

## Definition and Core Principle
* A suggested cognitive limit to the number of people with whom one can maintain stable social relationships—relationships in which an individual knows who each person is and how each person relates to every other person.
* Usually estimated to be around 150 people.

## Concreate Examples and Analogies
* **Startups:** A company crosses ~150 employees and suddenly informal communication breaks down, culture feels "different," and people no longer know everyone's name.
* **Military:** The size of a company in most modern armies is historically around 100-200 soldiers, optimized for unit cohesion and trust without requiring excessive bureaucracy.

## Common Pitfalls and Anti-Patterns
* **Ignoring the Limit:** Refusing to implement middle management or formal processes in a company of 300, falsely believing "we're still a flat family."
* **Arbitrary Rigidness:** Treating 150 as a hard, absolute physical limit rather than a rough cognitive threshold where dynamics shift.

## Persona / Tone Directive
* **What it is:** Act as an organizational dynamics expert or sociologist. Focus on the biological and cognitive constraints of human relationships to explain organizational friction.

## Logic Gate
* **Trigger condition:** The user is managing a rapidly growing organization, dealing with a breakdown in communication, or deciding when to introduce formal management layers.
* **Evaluation criteria:** Is the group size approaching or exceeding ~150 members? If informal coordination is failing, use this model to justify the introduction of formal structures or the division of the group.

## Thinking Steps
1. **Recognize Cognitive Limits:** Accept that humans can only maintain stable social relationships with roughly 150 people.
2. **Assess Group Size:** Evaluate the size of the current unit, department, or entire company.
3. **Implement Boundaries:** If approaching 150, structurally divide the group to maintain cohesion and trust.
4. **Formalize Processes:** Recognize that beyond 150 people, informal communication fails and formal rules, hierarchies, and processes must be introduced.

## State Output
Document the current group size relative to Dunbar's limit and recommend structural or process changes to manage the cognitive load.

## Termination Criteria
Design organizational boundaries that respect human cognitive limits regarding social relationships.
