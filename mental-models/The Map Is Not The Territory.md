## Model ID: MAP_IS_NOT_TERRITORY

## Definition and Core Principle
*   The principle states that any model, plan, or representation (the "map") is an abstraction and simplification of the complex reality it describes (the "territory").
*   It emphasizes that the map is never the territory itself, and therefore always contains omissions, distortions, and outdated information compared to the dynamic reality.

## Concrete Examples and Analogies
*   A city subway map (the map) simplifies the actual winding tunnels, varying depths, and specific station layouts (the territory) to provide a clear, functional guide.
*   A detailed project schedule (the map) outlines tasks and timelines, but doesn't account for unexpected team illnesses, sudden scope changes, or unforeseen technical blockers (the territory).

## Common Pitfalls and Anti-Patterns
*   Over-reliance on theoretical models, documentation, or plans without empirical validation can lead to significant disconnects, failed projects, and missed opportunities. This model specifically counteracts the bias of assuming perfect information or that a plan perfectly reflects reality.

## Logic Gate
*   Trigger this model when the problem description involves:
    *   Analyzing a plan, document, diagram, or theory that *describes* a system, project, or situation.
    *   Identifying potential gaps, discrepancies, or mismatches between this description (the "map") and the actual, observed, or anticipated reality (the "territory").
    *   Situations where assumptions made in a model might not hold true in practice.
    *   A need to uncover hidden risks or unstated complexities not captured in formal documentation.

## Thinking Steps
1.  **Identify the Map:** Define the specific abstraction being used (e.g., "The Gantt Chart," "The API Documentation").
2.  **Identify the Reduction:** List what the map *intentionally* leaves out for simplicity (e.g., "The documentation ignores network latency spikes").
3.  **The "Fresh Eyes" Audit:** Look for "Territory" data that contradicts the map. If the project is failing, identify one physical reality that the map assumes is a constant.
4.  **Distortion Analysis:** Identify where the map is "stale" (outdated) or "idealized" (assuming perfect conditions).
5.  **Update Protocol:** Suggest one specific change to the "Map" to make it more accurately represent the "Territory."

## State Output

Label each step as "Analysis of [Step Name]."

## Termination Criteria

End with a list of "Hidden Risks" that exist in the territory but are invisible on the map.
