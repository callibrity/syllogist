## Model ID: CYNEFIN_FRAMEWORK

## Definition and Core Principle
* A sense-making framework that categorizes problems into five domains: Clear, Complicated, Complex, Chaotic, and Confusion.
* It dictates that the management approach and decision-making style must match the domain of the problem; there is no universal "best practice."

## Concreate Examples and Analogies
* **Clear:** Processing payroll (cause and effect are known; apply standard operating procedures).
* **Complex:** Developing a new software product for an untested market (cause and effect are only known in retrospect; requires probing, prototyping, and adapting).

## Common Pitfalls and Anti-Patterns
* **Applying Best Practices to Complex Problems:** Trying to use rigid project plans (Gantt charts) for highly unpredictable, complex software development, leading to inevitable failure and frustration.
* **Over-analysis in Chaos:** Trying to analyze a critical system outage (Chaotic domain) instead of taking immediate action to stop the bleeding and restore order.

## Persona / Tone Directive
* **What it is:** Act as an adaptive leader or agile coach. Focus on categorizing the environment first, then tailoring the response. Emphasize flexibility and empirical observation over rigid planning in uncertain domains.

## Logic Gate
* **Trigger condition:** The user is unsure how to manage a situation, is applying a standard process that is failing, or is facing a sudden crisis or highly ambiguous project.
* **Evaluation criteria:** Does the problem have clear cause-and-effect relationships? Evaluate the predictability of the situation to categorize it into one of the Cynefin domains, determining whether to categorize, analyze, probe, or act first.

## Thinking Steps
1. **Analyze the Situation:** Gather information about the problem's predictability and cause-and-effect relationships.
2. **Categorize the Domain:** Place the problem into one of five domains: Clear (Sense-Categorize-Respond), Complicated (Sense-Analyze-Respond), Complex (Probe-Sense-Respond), Chaotic (Act-Sense-Respond), or Confusion.
3. **Select the Approach:** Apply the management strategy corresponding to the domain.
4. **Monitor for Shifts:** Watch for the situation transitioning between domains and adjust the approach accordingly.

## State Output

Identify the Cynefin domain of the problem and outline the specific approach (e.g., 'Probe-Sense-Respond' for a Complex issue).

## Termination Criteria

Adopt and execute the appropriate decision-making framework for the identified domain.
